Editorial Note

A Privilege to Feature

At Cosmopolitan Art Magazine Pakistan, we are deeply humbled and honoured to feature Mr. Rashid Rana, one of the most influential contemporary artists to emerge from Pakistan.

His pioneering contributions to visual art, education, curatorial practice and international cultural dialogue have transformed the landscape of contemporary art across the region and beyond.

About the Artist

Parallel Realities

Rashid Rana, with his pioneering digital works from Pakistan at the start of the 21st century and a creative practice spanning over three decades, is considered among the most influential visual artists in the subcontinent today.

His work has been exhibited at prestigious venues globally and is part of major collections including the British Museum, London, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York.

His oeuvre encompasses paintings, stainless steel sculptures, video installations, photo-sculptures and photo mosaics, with each medium becoming a site of inquiry into dualities, paradoxes and parallel realities.

Artist Statement

Identity, Duality & Location

Unburdening from self-imposed pressure of prescriptive and dogmatic ideas of affiliating one’s identity to political and cultural boundaries has been one of the core ideas in Rashid Rana’s practice.

His early interest in the duality of space expanded into a wider investigation of paradoxes, contradictions, polarities and parallel realities — a way of negotiating the burden of representing reality.

Rana considers the binaries of “actual” and “remote” more plausible than the overplayed binaries of East and West. His expression emerges through a negotiation between what is directly experienced and what is amassed indirectly across time and space.

“My art-making, exhibition-making and curriculum-making overlap and converge. To me, these different practices are all forms of production.”

Work Highlight

Everything and Nothing

In Everything and Nothing, thousands of European paintings from the 16th to the 19th century formulate a large image of two identical bookshelves — one sharp and one blurred.

The work creates a constant experience of registering something and its absence simultaneously, reflecting the limitation that exists between the translation of verbal and visual languages.

Legacy

Artist, Educator & Curator

Parallel to his art practice, Rashid Rana is known for his curatorial work and significant contributions to art academia in Pakistan.

As a founding faculty member and dean of the School of Visual Arts and Design at BNU, he has played a pivotal role in nurturing Pakistan’s next generation of artists while curating major projects such as the BNU Design Summit.

As founding chairholder of the UNESCO Chair on Inclusion through Art, he has actively promoted human diversity and inclusion through an expanded and transdisciplinary approach to creative practice.
