Exhibition · Lahore

Kinnaird College
Fine Arts Degree Show 2026

A celebration of emerging women artists in Lahore, bringing together works that move between memory, spirituality, identity, feminine experience, psychological tension and contemporary visual storytelling.

By Cosmopolitan Art Magazine Team · June 2026

The Fine Arts Degree Show 2026 by Kinnaird College opened at Ejaz Art Gallery, Lahore, bringing together a thoughtful body of work by graduating artists whose practices move between memory, spirituality, identity, feminine experience, psychological tension and contemporary visual storytelling.

The exhibition, which opened on Friday, June 5, 2026, presents the work of young artists including Yumnah Rizwan, Syeda Qurat Fatima, Maham, Samia Saleem, Amna Ghafoor and Raabel Mahboob. Together, their works reflect the creative depth and conceptual maturity of a new generation of Pakistani women artists.

What stands out in this year's display is the personal yet layered approach of the artists. Many of the works do not simply focus on visual beauty, but also open conversations around the unseen, the internal self, the body, resilience, spiritual presence and emotional transformation.

“The exhibition reflects how young women artists are using art to question, remember, heal and reimagine their surroundings.”

Faculty & Mentorship

The Fine Arts Degree Show 2026 reflects not only the dedication of graduating artists but also the guidance of faculty members and mentors who supported students throughout their academic journey.

Under the mentorship of Amra Khan (@khanamra) and Mizna Zulfiqar (@mizna.zulfiqar), students were encouraged to develop individual visual languages while engaging critically with contemporary artistic practices. Their guidance helped students refine their concepts, strengthen technical approaches and confidently present their final-year projects.

The diversity of themes explored throughout the exhibition — from spirituality and identity to memory, resilience and personal narratives — reflects an environment that values experimentation, research and artistic growth.
