Food & Culture · Lahore

ATALVI
Gelato

The handcrafted Italian gelato experience taking shape in Lahore — built around real flavour, craft, texture and founder Omer’s journey from one unforgettable scoop in London to a premium dessert space in Main Gulberg.

By Cosmopolitan Art Magazine Team

Feature Story

From London to Lahore

Some ideas begin with years of planning. Others begin with a single unforgettable scoop. For ATALVI Gelato founder Omer, that scoop came in London — pure, rich, smooth and memorable enough to spark a simple question: why not Lahore?

In Lahore’s growing dessert culture, where every new place tries to offer something sweet, ATALVI Gelato stands out with a quieter kind of confidence. It is not just about ice cream. It is about craft, flavour, texture, and the feeling of enjoying something made with intention.

Located in Main Gulberg, Lahore, ATALVI brings a premium Italian-style gelato experience to the city. With its promise of handcrafted luxury, real flavours and pure taste, the brand has created a dessert identity that feels refined, modern and rooted in quality.

“From London to Lahore, the soul remained the same: honest flavour, rich texture and a scoop that feels memorable.”

Meet the Founder

Omer’s Journey

Behind ATALVI is its founder, Omer, whose journey began with a scoop of gelato in London. It was the kind of gelato that stays with you — pure, rich and unforgettable.

That one experience sparked the idea of recreating the same feeling here in Pakistan, using authentic Italian recipes and real ingredients so people would not have to travel abroad to taste that kind of gelato again.

His training and passion for the craft helped shape ATALVI into more than a dessert shop. It became a space where Italian gelato tradition meets Lahore’s evolving appreciation for premium food experiences.

Founder: Omer Main Gulberg, Lahore Italian-Style Gelato Handcrafted

Brand Meaning

What Does ATALVI Mean?

One of the questions people often ask the brand is simple: what does ATALVI mean? In a Q&A session with the chef, the brand shared that ATALVI basically means “Italian” in Urdu. The name connects directly to the heart of the brand — Italian gelato recreated for Lahore with authentic recipes, real ingredients and a focus on pure flavour.

This makes the name feel especially fitting. ATALVI is not just a random brand name; it reflects the Italian inspiration behind the gelato and the idea of bringing that same rich, smooth and unforgettable experience to Pakistan.

The Q&A also explains what makes gelato different from regular ice cream. While ice cream is usually made with more cream, gelato is more milk-based, giving it a smoother texture and allowing the flavour to come through more clearly.

More Than Ice Cream

The Art of Gelato

Gelato is often confused with regular ice cream, but the experience is different. Ice cream is generally more cream-based, while gelato is more milk-based. This gives gelato a smoother texture, a denser feel and a flavour that comes through more clearly.

ATALVI understands that difference. The brand presents gelato as something more thoughtful than a quick dessert. It is a small experience — one that begins with the look of the scoop and continues through the texture, flavour and aftertaste.

From the first glance, ATALVI feels like a brand that cares about presentation. The clean interiors, premium feel, carefully styled gelato and elegant flavour combinations come together to create a dessert space that feels fresh for Lahore.

Behind the Craft

From Ingredients to Flavour

Every flavour begins long before it reaches the display cabinet. Fresh fruit is selected carefully, recipes are prepared from scratch, ingredients are balanced by hand and every batch is crafted with patience rather than shortcuts.

Fresh Preparation

Behind every scoop is a process built around real ingredients and careful preparation.

Real Ingredients

ATALVI’s flavour story begins with ingredients that feel fresh, rich and true to taste.

Balanced by Hand

Every ingredient is handled with care, allowing flavour, acidity and texture to come together.

Crafted Slowly

Flavours

A Brand Built Around Real Flavour

What makes ATALVI interesting is its focus on real taste rather than overly commercial sweetness. Their flavours feel crafted, layered and inspired by both international dessert culture and local preferences.

Some highlighted flavours include Crème Brûlée, Stracciatella, Black Forest, French Vanilla, Strawberry Cheesecake, Reese’s, Belgian Chocolate, Pistachio Kunafa, Mango and Coconut. Each flavour brings its own personality.

For our team at Cosmopolitan Art Magazine, the flavour that stood out the most was Pistachio Kunafa. It feels rich, textured and familiar, while still carrying the polished feel of a premium gelato flavour.

One-Year Journey

Taste, Trust & Return Visits

One of the strongest parts of ATALVI’s story is that it does not feel forced. The brand seems to have grown through genuine love for the product. People did not just come for a scoop; they came back for the experience.

In one year, ATALVI has built a recognizable presence in Lahore’s dessert scene. It has attracted gelato lovers, food bloggers, families, content creators and people looking for something more premium than everyday ice cream.

The brand’s growth shows how Lahore’s food culture is changing. Customers are now more open to handcrafted concepts, premium desserts and places that offer a complete experience instead of just a product.

Where Italy Meets Lahore

A Sweet Addition to Lahore’s Food Culture

ATALVI’s identity is strongly inspired by Italian gelato culture, but it does not feel distant from Lahore. Instead, it finds a way to bring that international inspiration into a city that already loves rich flavours and memorable food experiences.

This is especially clear in flavours like Pistachio Kunafa, Mango, Coconut, Belgian Chocolate, Black Forest and Crème Brûlée, where global technique meets familiar taste preferences. The result is a menu that feels exciting but still approachable.

For Cosmopolitan Art Magazine, ATALVI is worth highlighting because it represents the creative side of food culture. It shows how a dessert brand can use flavour, design, storytelling and craft to create something memorable.
