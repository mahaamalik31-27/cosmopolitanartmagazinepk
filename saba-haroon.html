About Saba

Born and raised in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, Saba Haroon grew up surrounded by architecture, monuments, and layered cultural aesthetics that deeply influenced her artistic language.

After moving to Pakistan, she pursued Fine Arts at the University of Punjab and later Interior Design at NCA, where she refined her approach toward installation art and spatial storytelling.

“I fold cranes as quiet extensions of myself. Each delicate fold holds time, patience, and a sense of presence.”

— Saba Haroon

Her work draws from Japanese philosophy and symbolism, particularly Wabi-Sabi and the legend of origami cranes representing healing, wishes, and transformation.
