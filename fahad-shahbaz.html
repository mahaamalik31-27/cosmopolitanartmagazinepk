From Youth Voice to Global Recognition: Fahad Shahbaz and the Rise of Youth General Assembly

A decade after its beginning, Youth General Assembly has grown into a 96-member assembly, creating space for young voices to engage with institutions, public issues, and leadership opportunities. According to the Youth General Assembly team, Fahad was recognised on the Forbes 30 Under 30 Asia 2026 list under Social Impact, marking an important moment for youth-led civic work in Pakistan.

For Cosmopolitan Art Magazine Pakistan, Fahad’s story sits within People & Impact because it reflects the kind of work that shapes society beyond one individual. His journey highlights leadership, representation, civic imagination, and the courage to create space where it did not exist before.

About Him

Fahad Shahbaz’s journey began with a simple but powerful idea: young Pakistanis deserve a real seat at the policy table. At eighteen, he started Youth General Assembly as a way to create meaningful youth representation and encourage civic awareness among the next generation.

Over the years, his work has grown beyond one initiative. YGA has become a platform where young people can understand governance, explore public service, develop leadership skills, and engage with issues that shape society. His journey reflects the importance of starting early, staying consistent, and building spaces where young voices are taken seriously.

Through Youth General Assembly, Fahad has helped build a platform around youth leadership, policy awareness, civic engagement, and social impact. His work speaks to a generation that wants to be heard, but also wants to take responsibility for shaping the future.

In Conversation

What inspired Fahad Shahbaz to begin Youth General Assembly?

Fahad began Youth General Assembly at the age of eighteen with the belief that young Pakistanis should have a stronger role in public and policy conversations. The idea was not just to create another youth platform, but to build a space where young people could learn, participate, and be represented with seriousness.

How has YGA grown since its beginning?

What started as one youth-led idea has grown into a 96-member assembly. Today, Youth General Assembly works around youth leadership, civic engagement, policy awareness, and creating stronger links between young people and institutions. Its growth shows how a consistent idea can turn into a structured movement.

Who can become part of Youth General Assembly?

Youth General Assembly is for young people interested in leadership, public service, policy, civic responsibility, social impact, and community change. It gives space to those who want to learn, contribute, and represent youth perspectives in meaningful conversations.

What makes Fahad’s journey important for young Pakistanis?

Fahad’s journey shows that young people do not need to wait for permission to begin creating change. By starting YGA at eighteen, he showed that leadership can begin early when there is clarity, purpose, and consistency. His work encourages other young Pakistanis to take their ideas seriously.

What does global recognition mean for this work?

According to the Youth General Assembly team, Fahad Shahbaz was recognised on the Forbes 30 Under 30 Asia 2026 list under Social Impact. For youth-led civic work in Pakistan, this recognition highlights how local ideas can reach global platforms when they are built with sincerity and long-term commitment.

Closing Note

Fahad Shahbaz’s story is not only about recognition; it is about beginning with an idea and building it into something that gives others a voice. Through Youth General Assembly, he has created a platform where young Pakistanis can see themselves as part of leadership, civic life, and national conversations.

His feature is a reminder that impact is not always created through one moment. Sometimes, it is built through years of consistency, responsibility, and the courage to create space where it did not exist before.
